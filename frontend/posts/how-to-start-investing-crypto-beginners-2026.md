# How to Start Investing in Crypto in 2026: A Beginner's Guide

Crypto investing has matured significantly since its early days. In 2026, regulated platforms, on-chain transparency, and structured staking plans make it easier than ever for new investors to participate — but the risks remain real and must be understood before committing any funds.

## Step 1: Understand the Risks

Before anything else, understand that:

- Cryptocurrency markets are highly volatile
- Returns are never guaranteed
- You may receive back less than you invested
- Regulatory changes can affect platform operations

Only invest money you can afford to lose. Read any platform's [Risk Disclosure](/risk-disclosure) thoroughly.

## Step 2: Choose a Regulated Platform

Look for platforms with FinCEN MSB registration, KYC requirements, and verifiable on-chain contracts. Unregulated platforms with no KYC carry significantly higher risk of fraud.

WolvCapital is registered in Palo Alto, California and requires full KYC verification for all accounts.

## Step 3: Complete KYC Verification

KYC (Know Your Customer) is a legal requirement for regulated platforms. You will typically need:

- A government-issued photo ID (passport or driver's licence)
- Proof of address (utility bill or bank statement)
- A selfie or live verification

This process usually takes 24-48 hours on regulated platforms.

## Step 4: Choose Your Investment Plan

Different plans suit different investor profiles:

- **New investors**: Start with a smaller minimum plan to understand the platform
- **Experienced investors**: Longer-duration plans with higher APY may suit larger allocations

## Step 5: Make Your First Deposit

Most regulated platforms accept USDT (Tether) or BTC. USDT is recommended for beginners as it is a stablecoin pegged to the US dollar, reducing exposure to crypto price volatility during the deposit process.
